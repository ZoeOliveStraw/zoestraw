My Story

I've been obsessed with games ever since I was a kid. I thought that video games were these magical, impossible worlds that lived in my TV and I loved exploring them. As a young child I filled countless graph paper notebooks with dungeon layouts, character drawings, descriptions of systems that I wanted to make. I spent all of my free time imagining the kinds of games that I might make if I were able to.

As I got older, some of the magic inherent to games gave way to a deeper, more mechanistic understanding of the underlying systems. Genres and trends filled out my understanding of a medium and, while I would always love games, I definitely never felt like I did as a child for a long time.

VR made me feel like a kid again, and ignited an immense passion.

I'll admit, before using one I thought that VR was another gimmick that would come and go. This was in 2017 when the original Vive had just come out. A friend had one and offered to let me try it. I popped on the headset and played a dungeon crawler, Vanishing Realms. I was immediately transported. It was incredible! Using my arms to notch the bow and arrows, the sheer degree of immersion provided by the HMD, the intuitive way you interacted with the world... It was incredible. I knew that this was what I wanted to work in, but I had no relevant skills.

It took me some time before I was able to take a long enough break to go to school. I had been working for many years at that point, totally not realizing my potential... But not I had dreams to aim for. I went to school for computer programmer and in my free time I spent every available second teaching myself game engines and skills that would let me create VR experiences. From simple 2D games up to VR games launched into headsets, I was able to get a job at my studio, Durham Mixed Reality Capture. While still studying, I was able to work with some amazing mentors and get experience in VR. I am so happy that I was able to have this opportunity while in school.
